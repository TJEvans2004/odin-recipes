Gumbo

Description

This gumbo is a hearty, soul-satisfying symphony of flavors. It’s a rich, savory stew where tender pieces of chicken and succulent sausage meld together in a deeply seasoned roux, creating a base that's both velvety and robust. The gumbo is a blend of spicy, smoky, and earthy notes, enhanced by the aromatic medley of onions, bell peppers, and celery, and simmered to perfection with a generous dose of Creole spices. The sausage adds a satisfying depth of flavor with its spicy kick, while the chicken remains juicy and flavorful. Each spoonful delivers a comforting warmth, with hints of garlic, bay eaves, and a touch of heat from the cayenne pepper. This gumbo is more than a dish; it's a celebration of rich, Southern tradition, served over a bed of fluffy white rice that soaks up every delicious bit of the thick, flavorful broth.

Recipe

  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 lb chicken thighs, bone-in, skinless, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 lb andouille sausage or smoked sausage, sliced into rounds
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 to 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup okra, sliced (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Gradually stir in the flour to make a smooth paste. Cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture turns a deep brown color (about 15-20 minutes). This is your roux, and it should have a nutty aroma.
  3. Once the roux is ready, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the chicken pieces and sausage slices to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the chicken starts to brown, about 5 minutes.
  6. Stir in the cayenne pepper, paprika, smoked paprika (if using), thyme, oregano, and bay leaves.
  7. Pour in the chicken broth and diced tomatoes, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  8. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce and soy sauce. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Simmer uncovered for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. If using okra, add it to the gumbo and simmer for an additional 15-20 minutes, until the okra is tender.
  10. Taste the gumbo and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Remove bay leaves. Stir in fresh parsley before serving.
  11. Spoon the gumbo over cooked white rice.
