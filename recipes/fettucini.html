Fettuccine Recipe

Description

Mom's fettuccine is a culinary masterpiece that embodies the warmth and love of home-cooked meals. The creamy, velvety sauce, rich with a perfect blend of Parmesan and fresh cream, clings lovingly to every strand of perfectly cooked fettuccine. Each bite delivers a harmonious symphony of flavors—subtle hints of garlic and a touch of nutmeg enhance the depth, while tender bits of chicken or sautéed mushrooms add satisfying texture. It’s not just a dish; it’s a comforting embrace that makes any meal feel special.

Recipe

  • Fettucine (12 oz)
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Chopped Parsley for Garnish

Instructions
  1. Make the pasta dough
  2. On a clean surface or in a large bowl mound the flour and make a well in the center.
  3. Crack the eggs into the well and add the salt and olive oil.
  4. Gradually mix the flour into the eggs with a fork, until the dough starts to come together.
  5. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour.
  6. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature.
  7. Roll out the dough
  8. After resting, divide the dough into two pieces.
  9. On a floured surface, roll out each piece into a thin sheet, about 1/16 inch thick. You can also use a pasta machine if you have one, starting with the widest setting and gradually moving to thinner settings.
  10. Cut the Fettuccine
  11. Once rolled out, lightly flour the dough sheets and roll them up loosely.
  12. Slice the rolled dough into 1/4-inch wide strips to create fettuccine.
  13. Unroll the strips and toss with a bit of flour to prevent sticking.
  14. Cook the Fettuccine
  15. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  16. Add the fettuccine and cook for 2-4 minutes, or until al dente. Fresh pasta cooks much quicker than dried pasta, so keep an eye on it.
  17. Drain the pasta and toss with a little olive oil if not serving immediately.
  18. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  19. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
  20. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally
