Chicken Fried Rice

Description

This chicken fried rice is a vibrant and savory dish, brimming with flavor and texture. Each bite offers tender pieces of chicken, perfectly cooked and infused with a hint of garlic and ginger. The rice is a glorious medley of golden grains, sautéed to a delightful crisp and tossed with a colorful mix of vegetables like peas, carrots, and green onions. Flavored with soy sauce and a touch of sesame oil, this chicken fried rice is both hearty and satisfying. Its well-balanced blend of ingredients creates a mouthwatering experience that’s both comforting and flavorful, making it the perfect meal for any time of day.

Recipe

  • 1 lb (450 g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups (450 g) cooked jasmine or long-grain rice (preferably cold or day-old)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup (150 g) frozen peas and carrots mix (or fresh, diced)
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2-3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add bite-sized pieces of chicken, seasoned with salt and pepper, and cook for 5-7 minutes until browned and fully cooked. Stir in minced garlic and ginger, cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil and heat over medium-high heat. Sauté finely chopped onion for 2-3 minutes until softened. Add a cup of frozen peas and carrots (or fresh, diced), and cook for another 2 minutes. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet.
  3. Pour beaten eggs into the empty side of the skillet. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked, then mix them with the vegetables.
  4. Add 2 cups of cold or day-old rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps and mixing well with the vegetables and eggs. Stir in the cooked chicken and its juices.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce (if using), and 1 teaspoon of sesame oil. Mix thoroughly and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. Stir in sliced green onions and cook for another minute. Serve hot.
